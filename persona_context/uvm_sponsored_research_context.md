# UVM Sponsored Research / Office of Sponsored Programs Context

## Role Overview
The UVM Office of Sponsored Programs (OSP) is the institutional office responsible for the administration of externally funded research. OSP provides pre-award support (proposal preparation, budget review, institutional sign-off) and post-award administration (compliance, reporting, financial management). No proposal can be submitted to an external sponsor without OSP authorization.

## Pre-Award Responsibilities
- Institutional sign-off: OSP (not the PI) signs all grant applications on behalf of UVM
- Budget review: ensure costs are allowable, allocable, and reasonable per sponsor rules and federal Uniform Guidance (2 CFR 200)
- Negotiation of sponsored research agreements, data use agreements, and material transfer agreements
- Review for conflicts of interest and export control compliance
- Verify cost sharing commitments are approved and documented

## Indirect Costs (Facilities & Administrative — F&A)
- UVM has a federally negotiated F&A rate with DHHS (Department of Health & Human Services)
- Rates vary by activity type: on-campus research, off-campus research, instruction, other sponsored activities
- Some sponsors cap or restrict F&A (NSF limits F&A on REU supplements; some foundations prohibit it)
- Waivers require OSP and sometimes central administration approval
- F&A is calculated on Modified Total Direct Costs (MTDC) — excludes equipment >$5K, subcontract amounts >$25K, tuition

## Budget Compliance (Uniform Guidance 2 CFR 200)
- **Allowable**: Costs must be permitted by the award and not prohibited by federal regulations
- **Allocable**: Costs must benefit the sponsored project
- **Reasonable**: Costs must reflect what a prudent person would pay
- Key cost categories: personnel (salaries, fringe), travel, supplies, subcontracts, participant support, equipment
- Prior approval requirements: budget reallocation, no-cost extensions, change in scope, change in PI

## Post-Award Administration
- Financial reporting: federal awards require periodic FFRs (Federal Financial Reports)
- Progress reports: technical/scientific progress reports per sponsor schedule
- Effort reporting: UVM uses a certification system for federal awards
- Closeout: final reports, financial reconciliation, data archiving, equipment disposition
- Audit risk: federal single audit threshold ($750K in federal expenditures/year)

## Subcontracts and Subawards
- Subawards to other institutions require prime sponsor approval and flow-down of applicable terms
- Subcontractors must certify compliance with federal requirements (debarment, lobbying, etc.)
- Fixed-price vs. cost-reimbursable subawards — each has different audit/documentation requirements

## Export Control
- Research involving certain technologies, materials, or foreign nationals may trigger Export Administration Regulations (EAR) or International Traffic in Arms Regulations (ITAR)
- Fundamental Research Exclusion (FRE) typically protects basic university research, but exclusions exist
- Foreign nationals on research teams must be screened
- Technology control plans may be required

## Conflict of Interest (COI)
- All PIs and key personnel must disclose significant financial interests
- NSF and PHS (NIH) have specific COI regulations
- UVM's COI policy applies to all externally funded research

## Common Issues OSP Flags
- Budget submitted to sponsor without OSP review — a policy violation
- Personnel effort underestimated relative to scope
- Cost sharing proposed without central approval
- Unallowable costs included (e.g., alcohol, entertainment, fines)
- Foreign components not disclosed to sponsor
- Student researchers: must be enrolled and may require additional insurance/liability coverage
- ORCA/intern projects using federal funds: special rules apply for participant support costs and stipends

## Vermont/UVM-Specific Context
- UVM RSENR (Rubenstein School), CDAE, and medical school are common research homes
- Vermont EPSCoR program (NSF) administered through UVM
- Vermont Agricultural Experiment Station (VAES) at UVM — USDA formula funds
- UVM is a land-grant institution — USDA/NIFA funding subject to specific rules
