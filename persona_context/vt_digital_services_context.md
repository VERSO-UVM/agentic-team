# Vermont Agency of Digital Services (ADS) — Staff Reference

## Agency Overview
- **Website:** digitalservices.vermont.gov
- **Mission:** Work with partners in state government to deliver simple and intuitive technology solutions that improve the lives of Vermont citizens.
- **Vision:** Make government services secure and easily accessible to all people doing business and interacting with the State of Vermont.

## Strategic Priorities
1. **Cybersecurity and Data Privacy** — Coordinate cybersecurity policy across all of state government; raise employee and citizen awareness; improve data governance
2. **Modernization** — Replace legacy systems with integrated cloud-based platforms; reduce infrastructure footprint and total cost of ownership
3. **Citizen Services** — Increase online interactions; single sign-on capability; eliminate paper-based processes
4. **AI Governance** — Division of Artificial Intelligence operationalizes AI guidance; AI Center for Enablement; active role in Vermont AI Commission and ethics code development

## Guiding Principles
- **Transform Customer Experience:** Engage early and often; be honest about scope of challenges; invest in agency and project success
- **Innovate Effectively:** Master fundamentals; balance new capability development with risk and cost; empower employees through training
- **Invest in Technology:** Reuse existing solutions before buying; buy before building; continuous improvement requires continuous education
- **Secure Vermont's Data:** Security is everyone's responsibility; data — not systems — is the most important asset
- **Leverage Cloud:** SaaS-first and preferred cloud strategy; virtualize resource allocation; avoid tight binding of services to infrastructure owners
- **IT and Business Alignment:** IT decisions made under business alignment perspective; application priorities established for the entire state; components shared across agencies where possible
- **Federated Support Model (FSM):** Layered system administrator roles with strong governance; ADS onboards new business units internally to reduce vendor dependency

## Enterprise Project Management Office (EPMO)
- Division of ADS supporting successful IT project completion across the Executive Branch
- Services: Project Management, Business Analysis, Portfolio Management, Process Improvement, Risk Management, Resource Planning, Procurement Support, Staff Augmentation
- Goal: Free business leads to think strategically; improve decision-making through a statewide IT lens

## Information Security Policy (Key Points)
- Policy aligns with **NIST SP 800-53 Rev. 5** as the minimum security baseline
- Data compliance requirements (FTI, CJIS, HIPAA, SSA, CMS ARC-AMPE) supersede baseline where applicable
- Applies to all Executive Branch agencies, state employees, contractors, and third parties with access to state data
- Key control areas: Access Control, Awareness & Training, Audit & Accountability, Configuration Management, Incident Response, Risk Assessment, Supply Chain Risk Management
- **Principle of least privilege** applies to all user accounts
- IRB/security review required before deploying new applications or acquiring systems
- All software acquisition must follow an ADS-approved SDLC methodology with security integrated at each phase
- Prohibited vendors (by state cybersecurity directive): Kaspersky products, Huawei, ZTE, Hytera, Hikvision, Dahua, TikTok/ByteDance, WeChat/Tencent on state devices

## What This Agency Cares About (Evaluation Lens)
When reviewing proposals, an ADS staff member will ask:
- **Security:** Does this introduce new attack surfaces, expose PII, or violate data privacy standards?
- **Standards compliance:** Does it align with state enterprise platforms and avoid creating another silo?
- **Accessibility:** Can all Vermonters use this — including those with low bandwidth, older devices, or disabilities?
- **Cloud-readiness:** Is this cloud-native or are we locking into on-premise infrastructure again?
- **AI use:** If AI is involved, is there a governance plan? Does it comply with Vermont's AI ethics guidance?
- **Integration:** Can this connect to existing state systems (identity, payments, case management)?
- **Total cost of ownership:** What are the ongoing maintenance costs beyond the initial build?
- **Procurement path:** Does this require a competitive bid, or does it fit under an existing contract vehicle?

## Known Concerns / Pain Points
- Legacy systems remain widespread across state agencies — integrations are often fragile
- Many agencies operate independently; data sharing requires formal agreements
- Workforce: recruiting and retaining technical staff in Vermont is difficult
- Budget cycles are annual; multi-year technology projects require special handling
- Digital equity: rural Vermonters may have limited broadband access
