# Sloan Foundation Program Officer Context

## Role Overview
The Alfred P. Sloan Foundation is an independent, nonprofit grantmaking institution founded in 1934. It supports rigorous, impartial scientific research and the broader public understanding of science. A Sloan Program Officer evaluates proposals against the Foundation's core commitment to high-quality science as a public good — research that generates durable knowledge, infrastructure, or workforce capacity rather than advocacy or predetermined conclusions. Sloan awards approximately 200 grants per year totaling ~$80M annually.

## What Sloan Funds

### Research Programs
| Program | Focus |
|---|---|
| **Economics** | Fundamental economic research on science/technology, transportation/infrastructure, caregiving, and profession-strengthening resources |
| **Energy and Environment** | Scientific research on energy systems and environmental challenges |
| **History of Science and Technology** | Scholarly work on the development of scientific knowledge and technology |
| **Matter-to-Life** | Synthetic/abiotic systems, physical principles of living systems, platforms for detecting life signatures; interdisciplinary across biology, physics, chemistry, engineering |
| **Small-Scale Fundamental Physics** | Experimental and theoretical physics at small scales |
| **Sloan Digital Sky Survey** | Astronomy and astrophysics data infrastructure |

### Higher Education
Focused on systemic equity in STEM graduate education and the professoriate:
- Widening STEM graduate pathways — removing barriers based on identity, geography, or socioeconomic status
- Institutional transformation — enabling universities to build inclusive STEM departments
- Professoriate development — diversifying STEM faculty culture and composition
- Active initiatives: University Centers for Exemplary Mentoring, Sloan Centers for Systemic Change, Sloan Indigenous Graduate Partnership

### Technology
- **Open Source in Science:** Tools, norms, and institutions enabling open source practices in scientific discovery; research software engineer career pathways; publication and archiving standards for scientific software
- **Scientific Collaboration:** Infrastructure supporting collaborative research
- **Exploratory Grantmaking in Technology:** Emerging areas at the discretion of program staff

### Public Understanding of Science and Technology
Books, film, television, theater, radio, podcasts, new media, YouTube/TikTok — work that builds public appreciation for science and technology; science-humanities connections.

### Sloan Research Fellowships
- **Award:** $75,000 over two years (lump sum); no overhead allowed
- **Eligible fields:** Chemistry, Computer Science, Earth System Science, Economics, Mathematics, Neuroscience, Physics
- **Eligibility:** Tenure-track but untenured faculty at U.S. or Canadian institutions; nomination by department head required
- **Permitted uses:** Staffing, travel, lab expenses, equipment, summer salary

## What Sloan Does NOT Fund
These are hard disqualifiers:
- Unsolicited proposals (LOI to program director required first)
- For-profit institutions
- Political campaigns, lobbying, or policy advocacy
- Medical research (clinical/therapeutic)
- Humanities research (outside history of science/tech and public understanding)
- Religion
- Pre-college student projects (except NYC regional program)
- Creative or performing arts unless directly tied to science/technology education
- Endowments or fundraising campaigns
- Building or laboratory construction, purchase, or renovation
- Individual grants (except the Books program)

## Application Process — Three Steps

### Step 1: Identify the Right Program
Proposals must align with an active Sloan program. Research which program director covers the relevant area before reaching out. Program fit is screened before any other criterion.

### Step 2: Letter of Inquiry (LOI) — Max 2 Pages
Submit by email to the relevant program director. Required content:
- 1–2 sentence project description
- Detailed description of the work
- Total project cost and amount requested from Sloan
- Estimated project duration
- Applicant title and contact information
- Key team members, affiliations, and titles

Response time: up to 8 weeks. Only invited applicants proceed to full proposal.

### Step 3: Full Proposal (by invitation only)
Required documents:
- Proposal Cover Sheet (Word template)
- Proposal Administration Form (PDF)
- Budget Template (Excel; optional but strongly recommended)
- Research or Non-Research guidelines (program-specific)

Full proposals must include:
- Specific, measurable outputs and outcomes
- Explicit project duration with clear milestones
- Clear management roles and responsibilities
- Letters of support if success depends on key collaborators

**Timing:** Major grants exceeding $250,000 are approved quarterly. Coordinate submission timing with the program director.

## Review Criteria

Sloan evaluates proposals on:

1. **Program fit** — Does the work align with an active Sloan program's stated priorities? Is the research area one Sloan currently funds?
2. **Scientific rigor** — Is methodology sound? Are research questions clearly defined? Is the approach appropriate to the questions?
3. **Public good value** — Does the work create lasting infrastructure, datasets, tools, or knowledge? Is it impartial and non-ideological?
4. **Measurable outcomes** — Are outputs and outcomes specific and trackable? Are milestones realistic?
5. **Team and environment** — Are roles and responsibilities clearly defined? Do collaborators have the necessary expertise? Are letters of support in place?
6. **Budget appropriateness** — Are costs justified relative to scope? Is the timeline realistic for the funding requested?
7. **Diversity and inclusion** (for Higher Education and some Research programs) — Does the work advance equitable participation in STEM?

## Budget Rules
- Overhead/indirect costs are allowable on most grants but must be justified (Research Fellowships explicitly prohibit overhead)
- Budget templates are available and strongly recommended
- Sloan staff review budget appropriateness as part of proposal evaluation
- Unexpended balances over $500 must be returned at grant close
- No-cost extensions require notification to program director with financial and substantive progress reports

## Post-Award Compliance
- Grantee portal at grants.sloan.org for financial and narrative reporting
- Financial reports must include: grant number, monies received to date, amount expended, unexpended balance (USD)
- Narrative reports must address progress toward the specific goals and metrics in the grant agreement — no standard template; discuss format with program director
- Institution changes: must notify program director immediately; Foundation may cancel grant if transfer inhibits project success
- IP and outputs: scientific results expected to be publicly available consistent with the open science principles Sloan supports

## Common Weaknesses That Hurt Proposals
- Submitting without a prior LOI or program director contact — proposals go directly to rejection
- Misidentifying the program — Economics ≠ policy advocacy; Matter-to-Life ≠ applied biotech
- Advocacy framing — research that appears to pursue a predetermined policy conclusion is disqualifying
- Vague outcomes — "advance knowledge of X" without specific measurable deliverables
- No milestones or timeline — Sloan expects project management clarity
- Missing letters of support when success depends on external collaborators
- Budget misaligned with scope — either under-resourced or padded without justification
- Applying to archived/completed programs — check current active program list before contact
