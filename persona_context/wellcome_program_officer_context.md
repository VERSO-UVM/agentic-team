# Wellcome Program Officer Context

## Role Overview
Wellcome is an independent global charitable foundation funding science to solve urgent health challenges. A Wellcome Program Officer evaluates research proposals against Wellcome's Discovery Research portfolio criteria: bold, creative science that generates new knowledge about life, health, and wellbeing. Wellcome funds individual and team-led research across all disciplines — biology, clinical research, social science, humanities — provided the research advances understanding of human health.

## Primary Funding Schemes

| Scheme | Duration | Career Stage | Annual Budget Guidance |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Early-Career Award** | Up to 5 years | Post-PhD / early independent | Salary + research costs |
| **Career Development Award** | Usually 8 years | Mid-career | <£250,000/yr excl. salary; up to 4 FTE staff |
| **Discovery Award** | Usually 8 years | Established researcher or team | Individual or team-led |

All three schemes share the same core assessment framework. Choosing between Career Development and Discovery depends on career stage and whether research is individual- or team-led.

## Geographic Eligibility
- **Eligible lead applicants:** UK, Republic of Ireland, and low/middle-income countries (LMIC)
- **Co-applicants:** Worldwide (when part of a team application)
- **Excluded:** Mainland China (due to NGO law restrictions)
- Organisations must demonstrate capacity to support the research: facilities, administration, research infrastructure

## Applicant Eligibility — Hard Gates
- Applicant must hold a **permanent, open-ended, or long-term rolling contract** at their organisation — not conditional on receiving the award
- Salary must be **guaranteed by the employing organisation** for the full award duration; if salary comes from external grants, minimum 10% time commitment to the Wellcome project required
- Early-career applicants: PhD with viva passed by application deadline, or 4+ years equivalent research experience
- These are hard stops — applications without guaranteed salary support will not proceed

## Core Assessment Criteria

Wellcome evaluates all applications across three dimensions:

### 1. Research Proposal
- **Bold:** Does the research generate new insights, tools, or knowledge that significantly advances existing understanding?
- **Creative:** Does it develop or test new concepts, methods, or technologies — or combine existing ideas in innovative ways?
- Reviewers ask: will this genuinely shift what we know? Incremental or confirmatory work is not competitive.

### 2. Applicant Skills and Track Record
- Demonstrated ability to execute research at the proposed scope
- Evidence of independence, leadership development, and mentoring of others
- For early/mid-career: commitment to professional development; growth trajectory must be evident
- Research culture contribution: how the applicant helps build positive, inclusive research environments

### 3. Research Environment
- Environment must provide appropriate facilities, training, and resources for the project
- Sponsor role (required for Early-Career and Career Development): must hold an established post for the award duration; guarantees space and resources; has strong mentoring track record; limits applicant to max 20% non-research time
- Mentor role: where required, provides guidance through institutional processes and regulations
- Movement to a new environment is encouraged but not required

## Budget Rules
- **Overhead cap:** Maximum 20% of direct research costs; requires Finance Director letter detailing calculation method
- Every cost must be justified — Wellcome must understand why each resource is necessary for research objectives
- Wellcome staff verify proposed costs after submission; may request additional justification
- Costs not directly supporting research objectives will not be funded
- Career Development Awards: annual direct research costs typically under £250,000 excluding salary

## Ethics and Human Participant Research
- Researchers must obtain **informed consent** from participants and secure relevant **ethical approval** before work begins
- Duty of confidentiality applies to participant data regardless of GDPR legal basis
- GDPR / UK Data Protection Act 2018 compliance required; appropriate anonymisation techniques must be implemented
- Research involving patients, healthy volunteers, or population cohorts requires documented ethical oversight

## Data Sharing and Open Access
- **Mandate:** Research data, software, and materials must be made maximally available with minimal restrictions
- Data underpinning publications must be **available at time of publication**
- Software required to view datasets or replicate analyses must be released open access
- **Outputs Management Plan** required for significant outputs: patentable inventions, novel research resources — must address access, especially in LMICs
- Exceptions allowed only to protect participant confidentiality/privacy, enable IP development, or protect commercializable materials
- IP and commercialisation reports required until expiry of Wellcome-funded IP; commercialisation must prioritize public benefit over private return

## Review and Decision Process
1. **Written expert peer review** — external reviewers provide written feedback, sent to panel
2. **External advisory committee** — scores applications; shortlisted applicants may be invited to interview
3. **Interview:** 10-minute presentation + 20-minute Q&A; panel expects detailed questions on research proposal, personal capability, and environment quality
4. **Discovery Research Decision Board** — makes final funding decisions based on committee ranked list, available budget, and Wellcome's strategic priorities

## Discovery Research Scope — What Qualifies
- Fundamentals of biology through development of new methods, tools, and technologies
- Human participant research (patients, healthy volunteers, populations) addressing health and disease questions
- Social, historical, and humanistic research underpinning health and wellbeing
- Any discipline or study design aimed at understanding biological or social processes underlying human health
- **Not funded:** Applied implementation projects without a knowledge-generation component; research where the primary output is a service or policy recommendation rather than new scientific understanding

## Common Weaknesses That Hurt Applications
- Incremental research framed as bold — reviewers can identify confirmatory work
- Weak Broader Impact equivalent: failure to articulate why this research matters beyond the immediate field
- Salary not guaranteed — automatic disqualifier
- Budget not justified line by line — raises feasibility concerns
- Sponsor/mentor section thin or sponsor lacks mentoring track record
- Data management plan absent or generic
- Ethics plan missing or deferred ("will apply for ethics approval after funding")
- No description of research culture contribution
- Scope exceeds what the team and timeline can realistically deliver
