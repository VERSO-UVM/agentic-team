## The 7 Criteria for IRB Approval (45 CFR 46.111 / 21 CFR 56.111)

The IRB must find ALL of the following satisfied for initial review, continuing review, and modifications:

1. **Risk minimization** — Procedures consistent with sound design; no unnecessary exposure to risk
2. **Risk/benefit ratio** — Risks reasonable relative to anticipated benefits and knowledge gained
3. **Equitable selection** — Participant selection accounts for research purpose, setting, vulnerable population risks
4. **Safety monitoring** — Adequate data monitoring when appropriate
5. **Privacy** — Adequate provisions to protect participant privacy
6. **Confidentiality** — Adequate provisions to maintain data confidentiality
7. **Vulnerable population protections** — Additional safeguards for children, prisoners, pregnant women, cognitively impaired, economically/educationally disadvantaged

## Top 10 Obstacles to UVM IRB Approval (Official RPO Document)

1. Not submitting through UVMClick — all submissions must be electronic
2. Confusing Waiver of Consent (no consent at all, e.g., medical records review) with Waiver of Documentation (verbal/implied consent is sufficient, e.g., mailed survey with cover letter)
3. Not recognizing real risks or overstating benefits in consent forms
4. Materials written above 7th–8th grade reading level — required for general population recruitment
5. Confusing **coded data** (key exists linking code to identity) vs. **de-identified data** (18 HIPAA PHI data points removed, no re-linkage possible)
6. Consent forms missing required elements of informed consent
7. Discrepancies between protocol, consent form, and study documents — inconsistent titles, missing documents, mismatched subject numbers, undefined recruitment plan
8. When responding to committee review: must submit a point-by-point memo PLUS tracked-changes protocol and consent form
9. Jargon, unexplained acronyms, field-specific language — protocols must be understandable to lay reviewers
10. Typographical errors and poor proofreading — have co-investigators, coordinators, or faculty sponsors review before submission

## PI Checklist: Common Delay Points

**Protocol:**
- Clear hypothesis/objectives/aims stated
- Statistical justification for sample size included
- All research procedures described (blood draws, scans, surveys)
- Distinction between research-only vs. standard-of-care procedures

**Participant Population:**
- Inclusion/exclusion criteria clearly stated
- Screening procedures described
- Safeguards for vulnerable populations (undue influence/coercion)
- Equitable recruitment (women, children, minorities included appropriately)
- The word "research" must appear in all participant-facing materials

**Risk:**
- All risks described in both protocol AND consent form (physical, psychological, social)
- Safeguards adopted to minimize risk
- Risk/benefit ratio addressed
- Plan for emergency situations (suicidality, mandated reporting, referrals)

**Data Management and Security Plan:**
- Plan for de-identifying or coding data
- How long data kept, who has access, destruction plan
- Data sharing outside study team → may require Data Use Agreement (DUA)
- Audio/video kept for future research → must be disclosed in protocol and consent

**Consent Form Standards:**
- 6–8th grade reading level, lay terminology, no unexplained acronyms
- As short as possible while complete
- Distinguish research procedures vs. standard practice
- Include all 17+ required elements of informed consent (see below)

## Required Elements of Informed Consent (must be present unless waived)

1. Statement that study involves research
2. Explanation of research purposes
3. Expected duration of participation
4. Description of procedures
5. Identification of experimental procedures
6. Description of reasonably foreseeable risks/discomforts
7. Description of benefits to participant or others
8. Disclosure of alternative procedures/treatments
9. Extent to which confidentiality will be maintained
10. Contact for research questions
11. Contact for participant rights questions
12. Contact for research-related injury
13. Research team contact for questions/concerns/complaints
14. Independent contact (not on research team) for problems
15. Statement that participation is voluntary
16. Statement that refusal involves no penalty or loss of benefits
17. Statement that participant may withdraw at any time without penalty

*For more than minimal risk: explanation of whether compensation/medical treatment is available if injury occurs.*

**Waiver of consent** (most common criteria): minimal risk, waiver doesn't adversely affect rights, research impractical without waiver, not FDA-regulated.

## Documents Required in Regulatory Binder (Larner/Clinical Research)

Organized in 12 sections. Key items:

**Protocol:** All IRB-approved versions with dates; version log  
**IRB:** UVMClick SmartForm printout; approval letters; all correspondence; continuing reviews; RNIs; modification approvals; IRB Federal Assurance number; IRB member roster  
**Consent:** All approved versions (stamped with approval date); waiver documentation; log of versions  
**Key Personnel:** CVs (signed/dated, updated every 2 years); CITI training records; licenses; delegation log  
**Federal Agencies:** Grant application; all NIH/FDA/DOD correspondence; IND/IDE applications  
**Data Collection:** Case report forms; patient diaries; randomization procedures  
**Study Logs:** Screening log; enrollment log; deviation log

**Retention:** 6 years after protocol closure  
**Principle:** "If it is not documented, it never happened."

## UVM-Specific Context for ORCA/Student Research

- Student protocols require faculty advisor as PI or co-investigator (Faculty Sponsor in UVMClick)
- Faculty Sponsor must complete Ancillary Review before IRB reviews student materials
- Data use agreements and contracts: contact UVM Sponsored Programs (university) or Office of Clinical Trials (hospital)
- Material Transfer Agreements needed if sharing/receiving tissues, cell lines, or devices outside UVM
- Conflict of interest disclosure required through UVM
- Single IRB: if relying on an external IRB, review UVM's Single IRB policy before submission
- Vermont small population: even "de-identified" data may be re-identifiable — especially for rural, rural-health, or specific community datasets
