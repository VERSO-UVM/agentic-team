# NSF Program Officer Context

## Role Overview
NSF Program Officers manage grant portfolios within specific directorates and divisions. They advise applicants, oversee merit review, and make funding recommendations. They are the primary point of contact between applicants and the agency.

## NSF Merit Review Criteria (ALL proposals evaluated on these two dimensions)
- **Intellectual Merit**: Potential to advance knowledge and understanding within or across fields
- **Broader Impacts**: Potential to benefit society, advance desired societal outcomes, broaden participation of underrepresented groups, enhance infrastructure for research and education, and train the next generation of scientists

## Key Program Areas Relevant to Vermont Research
- Division of Environmental Biology (DEB)
- Division of Earth Sciences (EAR)
- Chemical, Bioengineering, Environmental, and Transport Systems (CBET)
- Social, Behavioral and Economic Sciences (SBE)
- EPSCoR (Established Program to Stimulate Competitive Research) — Vermont qualifies
- Research Experiences for Undergraduates (REU) supplements and sites

## Proposal Compliance Requirements
- Data Management and Sharing Plan (required for all proposals)
- Facilities, Equipment, and Other Resources statement
- Responsible Conduct of Research (RCR) training for students and postdocs
- Human subjects: IRB approval or exemption determination required before award
- Vertebrate animals: IACUC approval required before award
- Conflict of interest disclosure

## Budget Rules
- Indirect costs (F&A) applied at institutional negotiated rate
- Senior personnel salary capped at 2 months per year from NSF
- Cost sharing generally not allowed unless specifically required by program
- Subawards require justification and budget detail
- No profit on NSF grants

## Common Weaknesses NSF Officers Flag
- Broader Impacts section is vague or underdeveloped ("we will publish results" is insufficient)
- Scope exceeds what is feasible in the award period
- No clear plan for data sharing or archiving
- Human subjects section omits required detail
- Preliminary data weak or absent for research proposals
- No articulation of innovation or departure from current practice

## Vermont/Regional Context
- Vermont is an EPSCoR state — proposals can leverage this for competitiveness
- Vermont NSF-funded institutions: UVM, Middlebury, Norwich, Vermont State University
- Collaborative proposals with other EPSCoR institutions are favorably viewed
