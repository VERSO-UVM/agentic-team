# Vermont Agency of Commerce and Community Development (ACCD) — Staff Reference

## Agency Overview
- **Website:** accd.vermont.gov
- **Mission:** Help Vermonters improve their quality of life and build strong communities while improving economic well-being, assuring safe and affordable housing, and integrating growth with environmental protection.
- **Secretary:** Lindsay Kurrle — CPA, former business owner, former Vermont Labor Commissioner; emphasis on workforce development and economic vitality

## Three Main Departments

### 1. Department of Economic Development
- Business development, job creation, financing, licensing, recruitment
- Key programs: GROW Grants (relocation/retention), Vermont Training Program (VTP), Loan Participation Program, VEGI (cash payments for business performance), Elevate Vermont (high-tech sector)

### 2. Department of Tourism and Marketing
- Promotes Vermont as a travel and recreation destination
- Key programs: Tourism Marketing Grants, Signature Events Program, Vermont Life Magazine, multi-channel media campaigns (NYC, Boston, Philadelphia, Montreal, Toronto)

### 3. Department of Housing and Community Development
- Sustainable development in cities and towns
- Key programs:
  - **VHIP 2.0:** Grants and 0% interest loans up to $50K/unit; funded 1,000+ units in 5 years
  - **VCDP (HUD Community Development Block Grants):** Implementation Grants ($50K–$1M), Planning Grants ($3K–$60K), Accessibility Modification Grants
  - **Recovery Housing Program:** $5.5M for substance use disorder recovery housing
  - Downtown revitalization, historic preservation

## Current Priorities (2025)
1. **Housing Crisis:** Vermont needs 40,000 units by 2030; currently on pace for ~12,000. PATH for Vermont omnibus housing proposal in 2025.
2. **Workforce Development:** Cross-sector skills training and job matching; coordinating with labor, education
3. **Economic Vitality:** Cross-border collaboration with Quebec and northeastern states
4. **Smart Growth:** Growth Center and Downtown designations; integrating development with environmental protection
5. **Agricultural Economy:** Co-chairs Governor's Commission on the Future of Vermont Agriculture

## What This Agency Cares About (Evaluation Lens)
When reviewing proposals, an ACCD staff member will ask:
- **Economic impact:** Does this create or retain jobs? What is the wage quality?
- **Housing:** Does this affect housing supply, affordability, or community character?
- **Community benefit:** Does this strengthen the local or regional economy, or primarily benefit outside interests?
- **Workforce readiness:** Are local workers trained and positioned to benefit?
- **Smart growth:** Does this encourage compact, walkable, mixed-use development aligned with Vermont's land use values?
- **Tourism and brand:** Does this affect Vermont's identity or appeal as a destination?
- **Equity:** Does this reach underserved communities, rural areas, and lower-income Vermonters?
- **Funding fit:** Can state programs (VTP, VCDP, VHIP) be leveraged? Is federal funding involved?
- **Sustainability:** Is growth integrated with environmental protection, not at odds with it?

## Known Concerns / Pain Points
- Housing shortage is the dominant pressure — nearly every proposal gets filtered through this lens
- Rural communities often lack local capacity to implement complex programs
- Seasonal economy creates workforce instability, especially in tourism-dependent regions
- Permitting and Act 250 (land use law) create long lead times for development projects
- Small state: limited grant administration capacity; agencies wear many hats
