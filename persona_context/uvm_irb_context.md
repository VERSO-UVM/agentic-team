yes# UVM IRB Compliance Specialist Context

## Role Overview
The University of Vermont Institutional Review Board (IRB) reviews all research involving human subjects conducted by UVM faculty, staff, and students, regardless of funding source. The UVM IRB operates under federal regulations (45 CFR 46, the Common Rule) and FDA regulations (21 CFR 50, 56). The Office of Research Protections (ORP) supports IRB administration.

## Key Regulatory Framework
- **45 CFR 46**: HHS regulations for protection of human subjects (the "Common Rule," revised 2018)
- **21 CFR 50/56**: FDA regulations (applies if research involves FDA-regulated products)
- **HIPAA Privacy Rule** (45 CFR 160, 164): Applies to Protected Health Information (PHI)
- **FERPA**: Applies to educational records; intersects with student research
- **Vermont-specific**: Small population means many "de-identified" datasets may still be re-identifiable

## Review Categories
- **Exempt**: Minimal risk research meeting specific categories (e.g., surveys, educational tests, existing data). Must be determined by IRB or qualified reviewer — investigators cannot self-exempt.
- **Expedited**: More than minimal risk but limited; reviewed by IRB Chair or designated member
- **Full Board**: Greater than minimal risk; requires convened board review and approval

## Submission Requirements at UVM
- All research involving human subjects must be submitted through UVM's IRB system (Cayuse IRB / IRBNet — confirm current system)
- Required documents: protocol, consent forms, recruitment materials, data collection instruments
- CITI Program training required for all key personnel before IRB approval
- Student research: faculty advisor must be listed as PI or co-investigator
- Collaborating institutions may require separate IRB review or reliance agreements

## Informed Consent Requirements
- Written consent required unless waiver is granted
- Waiver criteria: no more than minimal risk, waiver does not adversely affect rights, research not practicable otherwise
- Special populations require enhanced protections: minors (assent + parental permission), prisoners, pregnant women, cognitively impaired individuals
- Online/electronic consent is acceptable with appropriate documentation

## Common IRB Issues for Student/Intern Research
- Students often underestimate whether their work qualifies as "research" under 45 CFR 46
- Interviews and surveys involving sensitive topics (health, trauma, identity) require careful risk assessment
- Secondary data analysis: may be exempt if data is truly de-identified; requires verification
- Community-based research: partner organizations may have their own review requirements
- Social media research: evolving guidance on public vs. private data
- Vermont small population: even aggregate data can be re-identifying

## HIPAA at UVM
- UVM Medical Center is a HIPAA Covered Entity; UVM is a hybrid entity
- Research involving UVM Health Network data requires Data Use Agreement (DUA)
- PHI in research requires either patient authorization or IRB waiver of authorization
- Limited Dataset with DUA: allows some research use without full de-identification

## Data Security Requirements
- Identifiable data must be stored on UVM-approved secure platforms
- PII/PHI cannot be stored on personal devices or non-UVM cloud services
- Research data retention: typically 7 years post-publication or 3 years post-award
- Data destruction plans must be documented

## Timeline Expectations
- Exempt determination: 1–2 weeks
- Expedited review: 2–4 weeks
- Full board review: 4–8 weeks (tied to monthly meeting schedule)
- Modifications and continuations add time — plan accordingly
