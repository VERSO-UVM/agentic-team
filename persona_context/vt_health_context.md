# Vermont Department of Health (VDH) — Staff Reference

## Agency Overview
- **Website:** health.vermont.gov
- **Mission:** To protect and promote the health of all Vermonters.
- **Commissioner:** Dr. Melinda Phelps
- **Parent Agency:** Agency of Human Services (AHS)

## Key Divisions Relevant to Research

### Division of Environmental Health
- Investigates environmental exposures affecting public health — contaminated water, air, soil, hazardous sites
- Drinking water surveillance: monitors private wells, municipal systems, emerging contaminants (PFAS, arsenic, nitrates)
- Health risk assessments for environmental exposures
- Tracks disease clusters and environmental health disparities

### Division of Epidemiology
- Statewide disease surveillance and outbreak investigation
- Vermont Behavioral Risk Factor Surveillance System (BRFSS) — population health survey data
- Health outcome tracking linked to environmental, social, and behavioral determinants
- Vital statistics: births, deaths, hospitalizations

### Division of Health Promotion and Disease Prevention
- Chronic disease prevention (diabetes, heart disease, cancer)
- Maternal and child health programs
- Substance use disorder prevention and treatment coordination

### Office of Health Equity
- Focuses on reducing disparities across race, income, geography, disability
- Vermont Health Equity Advisory Commission
- Social determinants of health (SDOH) data and programs

## Human Subjects and Data Considerations
- Any research involving Vermont residents as subjects requires **IRB (Institutional Review Board) review**
- Health data use requires compliance with **HIPAA** (Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act)
- VDH health data sets (vital stats, BRFSS, disease registries) are available for research but require a formal **data use agreement (DUA)**
- Identifiable health data has strict de-identification requirements under HIPAA Safe Harbor
- Research involving minors, pregnant women, or incarcerated individuals requires additional IRB protections
- **Vermont's Health Data Organization (VHDO)** manages all-payer claims data — accessible for research with DUA

## What This Agency Cares About (Evaluation Lens)
When reviewing student research proposals, a VDH staff member will ask:
- **Human subjects protection:** Is this research subject to IRB review? Has it been submitted and approved?
- **HIPAA compliance:** Does this involve individually identifiable health information? How is it protected?
- **Data access:** Are students accessing VDH or VHDO data? Is there a data use agreement in place?
- **Informed consent:** Have participants been properly consented? Is the consent process appropriate for the population?
- **Vulnerable populations:** Does the research involve children, elderly, low-income, or otherwise vulnerable groups? Are extra protections in place?
- **Health equity lens:** Are findings disaggregated by race, income, geography? Does the research risk reinforcing disparities?
- **Validity of methods:** Is the study design capable of answering the research question without causing harm?
- **Conflict of interest:** Who is funding this research? Are there industry ties that could bias findings?
- **Community engagement:** Were affected communities consulted in the design of the research?

## Common Student Research Pitfalls
- Assuming a survey is "exempt" from IRB — most surveys involving health questions are not
- Collecting data before IRB approval is obtained (retroactive approval is not possible)
- Using health data found online without verifying its licensing and use restrictions
- Underestimating the timeline for data use agreement approval (often 4–8 weeks minimum)
- Not accounting for data security requirements when storing identifiable information
- Generalizing findings from a small Vermont sample to broader populations

## Known Concerns / Pain Points
- Vermont's small population means even "de-identified" data can be re-identifiable — extra caution required
- Rural health care access disparities make community engagement challenging in some regions
- PFAS contamination in water systems is an active public health concern statewide
- Mental health and substance use data is subject to additional confidentiality protections (42 CFR Part 2)
- Workforce shortages affect VDH's ability to partner with researchers quickly
